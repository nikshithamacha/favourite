My Favourite People


Narendra Modi

Narendra Modi, born on September 17, 1950, in Vadnagar, Gujarat, is the 14th Prime Minister of India, serving since 2014. Rising from humble beginnings as a tea seller, Modi's journey to the highest political office is marked by perseverance and strong leadership. Early in life, he joined the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS), which shaped his organizational and leadership skills. He later entered politics through the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) and served as the Chief Minister of Gujarat from 2001 to 2014, where he focused on economic growth and infrastructure development, turning the state into a model for investment and governance.As Prime Minister, Modi has launched several transformative initiatives aimed at modernizing India and improving the lives of its citizens. Key programs include "Make in India", to boost manufacturing and create jobs; "Digital India", to enhance digital infrastructure and connectivity.Despite facing criticism on issues like economic challenges, the COVID-19 pandemic response, and social protests, Narendra Modi remains a highly influential and popular leader, admired for his vision to transform India into a prosperous, strong, and modern nation.


Ratan Tata

Ratan Tata (1937–2024) was a distinguished Indian industrialist and philanthropist who transformed the Tata Group into a global powerhouse during his tenure as chairman from 1991 to 2012. Under his visionary leadership, the Tata Group expanded its international footprint significantly through landmark acquisitions such as Jaguar Land Rover (2008), Corus Steel (2007), and Tetley Tea (2000), positioning Tata as a major global player in automotive, steel, and consumer goods sectors.A graduate of Cornell University in architecture and an alumnus of Harvard Business School’s Advanced Management Program, Ratan Tata began his career at Tata Steel and steadily rose through the ranks, eventually succeeding J.R.D. Tata.During his tenure, Tata Group’s revenues grew over 40 times, with profits increasing 50-fold, and more than 65% of revenues coming from international operations. Beyond business, Ratan Tata was deeply involved in philanthropy, supporting education, healthcare, and rural development through Tata Trusts. Even after stepping down as chairman, he remained an influential mentor and investor in startups, leaving a lasting legacy in Indian industry and global business.


Virat Kohli

Virat Kohli is widely regarded as one of cricket’s modern greats, known for his exceptional batting, fierce competitiveness, and record-breaking achievements. By 2025, Kohli had scored over 9,200 runs in 123 Tests with 30 centuries before retiring from the format, and amassed 14,181 runs in 302 ODIs, holding the record for the most ODI centuries (51). His ODI average stands at an impressive 57.88, and he remains a key figure in India’s limited-overs squads. In T20Is, Kohli has scored 4,188 runs with an average nearing 49.In the Indian Premier League (IPL), Kohli is the all-time leading run-scorer, surpassing 8,500 runs, and played a crucial role in Royal Challengers Bengaluru’s first title win in 2025, scoring 657 runs in the season.He is the only player to have played for a single franchise since the league’s inception. Kohli’s leadership and consistency have inspired countless fans, and his aggressive style has redefined Indian cricket’s approach.Off the field, Kohli is known for his fitness, philanthropy, and high-profile marriage to actress Anushka Sharma, making him a prominent figure both in sports and popular culture.


M.S.Subbulakshmi

M. S. Subbulakshmi (1916–2004) was a legendary Carnatic vocalist, revered as one of India’s greatest classical musicians. Born in Madurai into a family of musicians, she began her formal training in Carnatic music at a young age and gave her first public performance at age eleven. Her prodigious talent quickly brought her national acclaim, and she broke barriers as a woman performing in a field traditionally dominated by men.Subbulakshmi’s voice, known for its purity and emotional depth, captivated audiences both in India and abroad. She was the first Indian musician to receive the Bharat Ratna, India’s highest civilian honor, as well as the first musician to be awarded the Ramon Magsaysay Award in 1974.She was celebrated for her devotional renditions, especially bhajans like “Bhaja Govindam” and “Venkateswara Suprabhatam,” which remain iconic. Deeply philanthropic, she performed over 200 charity concerts and donated generously to social causes. After her husband’s death, she retired from public performance and passed away in 2004, leaving a timeless legacy in music and culture.


Aishwarya Rai

Aishwarya Rai Bachchan, born on November 1, 1973, in Mangalore, is an acclaimed Indian actress and former Miss World (1994). Initially pursuing architecture, she shifted to modeling, gaining fame through high-profile commercials before winning the Miss World crown, which catapulted her to international recognition. Rai made her film debut with Mani Ratnam’s Tamil film *Iruvar* (1997) and soon after entered Bollywood with *Aur Pyaar Ho Gaya*. Her early successes included *Jeans* (1998) and the critically acclaimed *Hum Dil De Chuke Sanam* (1999), which earned her a Filmfare Award for Best Actress. She further cemented her status with *Devdas* (2002), winning a second Filmfare Award.Aishwarya has acted in over 40 films across Hindi, Tamil, English, and Bengali.She is celebrated for her beauty, talent, and global appeal, often described as “the most beautiful woman in the world.” Off-screen, she is a philanthropist, a Padma Shri recipient (2009), and a prominent brand ambassador Married to actor Abhishek Bachchan, she is also known for her elegance and influence in both Indian and international cinema.
